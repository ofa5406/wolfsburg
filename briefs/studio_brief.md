## Concept

This studio engages with the international competition **Urban Vision Wolfsburg 2026** as a framework to explore contemporary urban challenges and future-oriented design approaches.

The studio focuses on the integration of **artificial intelligence (AI) into urban design processes**, positioning prompting as a central method of conceptualization, production, and representation.

Rather than treating AI as a tool, the project investigates how it can actively shape the way we conceive, design, and communicate urban environments. The studio is a testing ground for a new paradigm: the **Prompt City** — where urban ideas emerge through iterative dialogue between human intention and machine intelligence.

---

## Methodology

The project emphasizes a **prompt-based design methodology**, integrating AI into all phases:

- Concept development through iterative prompting
- Urban and architectural design using generative systems
- Development of computational tools or custom applications
- AI-supported visualization and representation
- Critical reflection on AI as a design partner

In the first phase of the semester, students develop their own applications in an urban context using tools such as **Claude Code**, Codex, and other programming agents.

---

## Possible Directions

Projects may address a wide range of urban issues in Wolfsburg and beyond. Possible directions:

- AI-driven urban analysis and scenario generation
- New forms of participatory or responsive urban systems
- Data-informed spatial strategies and adaptive environments
- Hybrid physical-digital urban spaces
- Critical perspectives on automation, authorship, and control in urban design

Combinations of different approaches are strongly encouraged.

---

## Requirements

- Teams of **3 students** (larger groups not allowed)
- Final submission is a **comprehensive design proposal** consisting of:
  - Conceptual and analytical material
  - Urban and/or architectural design (alternative mediums: prototypes, tools, or applications addressing an urban issue)
  - Visualizations and representations
  - A short reflection on the design process
  - Presentation

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Clear and consistent concept based on a specific urban issue | 20% |
| Meaningful integration of AI into the design process (or other computational methods) | 20% |
| Quality of the design (spatial, urban, and aesthetic qualities of the developed artefacts) | **50%** |
| Reflection on the role of AI in urban design | 10% |

## Working Process

Working with AI tools and custom workflows takes time to get right:
- Iteratively develop your design
- Regularly test and refine your prompts
- Coordinate your work with the teaching staff

Starting late with the tools will show in the final work.

# 03 — Mobility / Transfer Hub Typologies: Theory & Precedent

> Knowledge base for defending the **WHY** of the Wolfsburg 68-hub, three-tier network (6 Large / 19 Medium / 43 Small). Use this to back up hub spacing, the element palette per tier, the three-zone arrival sequence, and the shared design language with established standards and built precedents.

---

## 1. What a mobility hub is

A **mobility hub** is a recognisable place where several ways of moving — public transport, walking and cycling, and *shared* transport (car-share, bike-share, e-scooters, future shuttle/pod modes) — are deliberately brought together at one point, often with extra everyday services (parcel lockers, seating, info, sometimes shops or community space). It is an **interchange you can read as a place**, not just a stop.

- CoMoUK (the UK national shared-transport charity) defines hubs as *"interchanges where public transport, active transport (cycling and walking), and shared transport (car clubs, bike share and future modes such as e-scooters) come together, sometimes along with community facilities."* [CoMoUK – Introducing the mobility hubs concept](https://www.como.org.uk/news/introducing-the-mobility-hubs-concept)
- The Shared-Use Mobility Center frames them as *"where people go to move"* — physical anchors that combine transportation function with **placemaking**. [SUMC – Mobility Hubs: Where People Go to Move (2019)](https://sharedusemobilitycenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/Mobility-Hubs_SUMC_Web.pdf)
- A hub bundles **junction points where various transport offers and additional services are concentrated at one location**, the framing used by Munich's MVG. [VCD – MVG more München](https://www.vcd.org/themen/multimodalitaet/beispiele/mvg-more-muenchen)

**Why this matters for Wolfsburg:** our thesis converts car-land into public life. The hub is the device that does the conversion — it absorbs the mobility function that parking used to scatter across the street, frees the land, and gives back a *place*. This is exactly the SUMC "hub as place / catalyst" logic, and it is what separates our scheme from a bus-stop upgrade.

## 2. The tiered-network concept

Hubs are **not one-size-fits-all**. Every serious programme organises them into a **tier/typology hierarchy** sized to location, density and the number of modes served. A small set of large interchanges anchors the system; a denser carpet of small hubs guarantees that *everyone* has one within an easy walk.

**CoMoUK** structures its guidance around **six hub typologies** that vary by location and use, from large city-centre interchanges down to "mini" stations for suburban/rural areas. [CoMoUK – Introducing the mobility hubs concept](https://www.como.org.uk/news/introducing-the-mobility-hubs-concept) · [Intertraffic – Mobility hubs: the multimodal stations at the centre of everything](https://www.intertraffic.com/news/infrastructure/mobility-hubs-multimodal-stations-at-the-centre-of-everything)

The **UK Government / CoMoUK placemaking guidance** sorts hubs into four types by mode-count and component complexity — a clean precedent for our S/M/L logic:

| Gov.UK / CoMoUK hub type | Where it goes | Modes & components |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 modes, **core** components | lower-density residential | smallest footprint |
| 1–2 modes, **extra** components | high streets, village centres | + WiFi, water, cycle repair |
| 3+ modes, **more** components | local/suburban centres, rail stations | + toilets, baby-change |
| 3+ modes, **commercial uses** | town centres, universities, tourist destinations | largest, with retail/community |

[GOV.UK – Delivering quality: mobility hubs](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/sustainable-placemaking-guidance-for-mobility-hubs/delivering-quality-mobility-hubs-html)

**Mapping to our scheme:** our **Large** hubs ≈ Gov.UK "3+ modes / commercial uses" (gastronomy, shops, community, housing above); **Medium** ≈ "3+ modes / more components"; **Small** ≈ "1–2 modes / core components." This is a defensible one-to-one correspondence with national guidance.

## 3. How spacing & walking-catchment is determined

The governing principle is the **walkable catchment**: a hub must sit within an easy walk of the homes it serves, so the *first/last leg is on foot*, not by car.

- **CoMoUK** evaluates candidate sites by the number of people living **within 800 m / ~10 minutes' walk**, plus visibility on approach and good walking/cycling access. [CoMoUK – Introducing the mobility hubs concept](https://www.como.org.uk/news/introducing-the-mobility-hubs-concept) · [ST14 Mobility hubs guidance, CoMoUK (Winchester Local Plan)](https://www.localplan.winchester.gov.uk/assets/inline/1051/ST14-Mobility-hubs-guidance-CoMoUK.pdf)
- The **Gov.UK placemaking guidance** repeats the **800 m / 10-minute** standard as the reference catchment for masterplanning. [GOV.UK – Delivering quality: mobility hubs](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/sustainable-placemaking-guidance-for-mobility-hubs/delivering-quality-mobility-hubs-html)

**The 800 m figure as a design ceiling, not a target.** 800 m / 10 min is the *outer* acceptable walk to a multimodal interchange. Our tiers deliberately work *inside* that envelope:

| Our tier | Walk radius (our design) | Rationale vs. the 800 m norm |
|---|---|---|
| **Large** (6) | 700–1000 m | At/around the CoMoUK 800 m interchange catchment — these are the big anchors people will walk further to reach. |
| **Medium** (19) | 300–500 m | ~3–6 min walk — denser than the interchange norm, so a richer multimodal offer is always nearby. |
| **Small** (43) | 150–250 m | ~2–3 min walk — a fine grain that guarantees a micromobility/dwelling point on practically every block. |

**The defensible argument:** the three radii are *nested*. The Large hubs satisfy the 800 m interchange standard; the Medium and Small tiers then **over-deliver** access, so no resident is ever far from *some* hub, and the system degrades gracefully (miss your nearest Small hub, the next is one block on). This is the same "anchor + fine grain" structure CoMoUK and SUMC describe, tuned tighter because we are designing a *post-car* district where walking is the default first leg.

## 4. International precedents

### 4.1 Bremen — *mobil.punkt* / *mobil.pünktchen* (Germany)
The foundational European precedent for a **tiered car-share hub network** in public space, running since 2003.

- **Two tiers by vehicle count:** *mobil.punkt* = larger stations with **4–12 car-share vehicles**, sited next to tram/bus/rail with taxi stands and neighbourhood facilities; *mobil.pünktchen* = small neighbourhood stations with **2–3 vehicles** dropped into streets with high parking pressure. [SmartHubs wiki – mobil.punkte & mobil.pünktchen Bremen](https://data.smartmobilityhubs.eu/wiki/Networks/mobil.punkte_and_mobil.p%C3%BCnktchen_Bremen)
- **Scale:** as of Nov 2024, **51+** stations, roughly **one-third of all car-share stations** in Bremen. [Bau Bremen – mobil.punkte und Carsharing](https://bau.bremen.de/mobilitaet/nachhaltige-mobilitaet/mobil-punkte-und-carsharing-31612)
- **Hard outcome:** the demand reduction equals roughly **32 km of street-space relief**; achieving the same relief with multi-storey car parks would cost *"significantly over €100 million."* [Bau Bremen – mobil.punkte und Carsharing](https://bau.bremen.de/mobilitaet/nachhaltige-mobilitaet/mobil-punkte-und-carsharing-31612)
- **Access rule, not just an offer:** providers must meet the **Blue Angel** environmental criteria *and* prove significant parking relief to get a station — a governance lesson, not only a design one. [VCÖ – mobilpunkt und Car-Sharing-Aktionsplan Bremen](https://mobilitaetsprojekte.vcoe.at/mobilpunkt-und-car-sharing-aktionsplan-der-stadt-bremen)

**What worked / transfer:** Bremen *proves* the tier logic (big-at-transit + small-in-streets) and gives us the headline economic argument — **hubs relieve far more street than car parks, far cheaper.** Bremen's co-author Rebecca Karbaumer also co-wrote the *Shared Mobility Rocks* planner's guide, so the design thinking is documented. [Share North – the Guide](https://share-north.eu/the-guide/)

### 4.2 Coventry / West Midlands (UK) — *Local Travel Points*
UK's first network of branded mobility hubs, built from **modular components**.

- *"A collection of modular components which can be assembled to reinvent how we use parking bays and small public spaces"* — explicit **kit-of-parts** thinking. [WMCA – New mobility hub set for trial](https://www.wmca.org.uk/news/new-mobility-hub-set-for-trial-on-the-streets-of-the-west-midlands/)
- **Elements:** cycle hire, secure bike hangars, e-scooters, e-cargo bikes, car-club + EV charging, wayfinding maps, real-time PT info, tool/repair stations, seating and greenery, parcel lockers. [TfWM – Mobility hubs](https://www.tfwm.org.uk/who-we-are/our-strategy/innovation-and-future-transport/mobility-hubs/)
- **Identity & placemaking:** community engagement via Commonplace plus **commissioned public art** (a Black Country artist co-made a sculpture with residents). Two Halesowen hubs earned **CoMoUK "Gold"** accreditation. [TfWM – Mobility hubs](https://www.tfwm.org.uk/who-we-are/our-strategy/innovation-and-future-transport/mobility-hubs/)

**Transfer:** strongest precedent for our **kit-of-parts + identity marker** approach, and for converting *parking bays* directly into hubs.

### 4.3 Bergen & Oslo (Norway)
- Bergen opened Norway's first *mobilpunkt* in May 2018 and now hosts ~**10 hubs**; each combines **reserved (mostly electric) car-share bays + secure bike parking + city-bikes + EV charging**, sited next to PT and ride-share stops. [Share North – Bergen's first Mobilpunkt](https://share-north.eu/2018/05/bergen-celebrates-the-grand-opening-of-the-citys-first-mobilpunkt/) · [Share North – Bergen, a city dedicated to mobility hubs](https://share-north.eu/2019/07/bergen-a-city-dedicated-to-mobility-hubs-emissions-reduction-and-transnational-learning/)
- Both cities developed hubs under the EU **Interreg SHARE-North** programme; Oslo's Ruter targets an **emission-free** fleet by 2028. [Business Norway – Norway pioneers smart, green urban mobility](https://businessnorway.com/articles/norway-pioneers-smart-green-urban-mobility)

**Transfer:** the **electric, transit-adjacent core** for our hubs, and a peer network we share the SHARE-North evidence base with.

### 4.4 Minneapolis (USA)
- 2019 pilot: **12 hubs**, three months, each with a **bus stop, bench, designated bike-share & e-scooter parking, and wayfinding signage with travel times** to nearby destinations — physical + programmatic + digital layers. [SUMC – Mobility Hubs (Minneapolis pilot doc)](https://opencommons.org/images/f/f4/Wcmsp-224822.pdf) · [Smart Cities Dive – Minneapolis pilots mobility hubs](https://www.smartcitiesdive.com/news/minneapolis-pilots-mobility-hubs/564026/)
- **Result:** **64% of users** said the improvements made them *more likely* to use the hub's transport options; **transit-centric hubs out-performed** those placed only on bike infra — proximity/visual link to the transit stop was decisive. [City of Minneapolis – Mobility Hubs](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/programs-initiatives/transportation-programs/mobility-hubs/)

**Transfer:** evidence that **wayfinding-with-travel-times** and **siting on transit** drive usage — directly supports our Zone 2 real-time info and our anchoring of Large hubs on autonomous-bus lines.

### 4.5 Munich — MVG *more* (Germany)
- MVG *more* bundles **bike-share (MVG Rad), e-bikes, car-share, PT, taxi and EV charging** at physical **Mobilitätsstationen** commissioned by the City of Munich. [VCD – MVG more München](https://www.vcd.org/themen/multimodalitaet/beispiele/mvg-more-muenchen) · [MVV – Shared Mobility](https://www.mvv-muenchen.de/mobilitaetsangebote/shared-mobility/index.html)

**Transfer:** a large-city operator model — one recognisable brand across many physical stations — supporting our **single design language across 68 hubs**.

### 4.6 Vienna — *WienMobil Stationen* (Austria)
- Rolling out since 2018: every station has **WienMobil Rad (bike-share) as the baseline**, with many adding **WienMobil Auto (e-car-share)**, e-scooters, **bike service stations**, and bike/parcel boxes. [Wiener Linien – WienMobil Stationen](https://www.wienerlinien.at/wienmobil/stationen) · [NRW.URBAN – Sharing meets Öffis](https://nrw-urban.de/sharing-meets-oeffis-die-wienmobil-stationen-der-wiener-linien/)

**Transfer:** the clearest **"baseline element + add-ons by tier"** precedent — bike-share is the constant; everything else scales up — exactly our Small→Large element logic.

### 4.7 Copenhagen / Greater Copenhagen (Denmark)
- Research-led, regional approach (ShareDiMobiHub / living labs): **flexible micro-hubs at suburban stations** combining e-bikes, car-share and EV charging under **modular solar canopies**, with **wayfinding pylons (pictograms + LED) visible from ~100 m** and **permeable paving / green swales** for stormwater. [Interreg – ShareDiMobiHub & EU Shared Mobility guidelines](https://www.interregnorthsea.eu/sharedimobihub/news/european-commissions-expert-group-shared-mobility-guidelines-and-greater) · [Intertraffic – At the hub of everything](https://www.intertraffic.com/news/urban-mobility/at-the-hub-of-everything)

**Transfer:** direct precedent for our **vertical beacon canopy** (their pylon visible from 100 m ≈ our beacon readable from 50 m) and for **plug-and-play modular reconfiguration** — the kit-of-parts proposition.

## 5. The logic of urban configuration

### 5.1 Siting: density, transit, destinations
Three siting drivers recur across all precedents and standards:

1. **Density of homes within the walk catchment** (CoMoUK's 800 m / 10-min test). [CoMoUK](https://www.como.org.uk/news/introducing-the-mobility-hubs-concept)
2. **Proximity & *visual* link to high-quality transit** — Minneapolis showed transit-anchored hubs simply work better. [City of Minneapolis](https://www.minneapolismn.gov/government/programs-initiatives/transportation-programs/mobility-hubs/)
3. **Destinations & footfall** — high streets, centres, stations, campuses; visibility on the approach. [GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/sustainable-placemaking-guidance-for-mobility-hubs/delivering-quality-mobility-hubs-html)

**For Wolfsburg:** Large hubs land on **repurposed multi-storey car parks** — already structurally sized, centrally located, and transit-adjacent — converting the single biggest car-land asset into the network's anchors. Medium and Small hubs then fill the residential grain inside the catchment.

### 5.2 Hub as catalyst (place, not stop)
The defining move is to design the hub **as a public space**. SUMC: hubs are *"where people go to move."* The *Shared Mobility Rocks* guide is explicit that *"mobility hubs should be designed as public spaces for the people using them … providing a welcoming experience while maintaining the core function,"* and that hub land can host **festivals, farmers' markets, gardens, art, cafés or community space.** [SUMC](https://sharedusemobilitycenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/Mobility-Hubs_SUMC_Web.pdf) · [Share North – the Guide](https://share-north.eu/the-guide/)

This is the engine of our thesis: at Large hubs the ground floor becomes **gastronomy/shops/community** and the upper floors **housing** — the hub *is* the new public life on the freed car-land.

### 5.3 The three-zone arrival sequence
Our **Zone 1 Vehicle Edge → Zone 2 Threshold/decision (real-time info) → Zone 3 Dwelling Edge (seating)** mirrors how the precedents organise arrival, even where they don't name it:

- NACTO's principle: keep street furniture in the **buffer between curb and the pedestrian through-zone**, preserving a clear walking path — i.e. a structured **vehicle-edge → buffer → walk/dwell** gradient. [NACTO – Bike and Scooter Parking](https://nacto.org/publication/urban-bikeway-design-guide/maintenance-and-operations/bike-and-scooter-parking/)
- Minneapolis put **real-time / travel-time wayfinding at the decision point** and **seating for dwelling** — our Zone 2 and Zone 3. [Smart Cities Dive – Minneapolis](https://www.smartcitiesdive.com/news/minneapolis-pilots-mobility-hubs/564026/)
- Copenhagen's **pylon at the threshold** orients first-time users before they choose a mode — our Zone 2 beacon + info. [Intertraffic – At the hub of everything](https://www.intertraffic.com/news/urban-mobility/at-the-hub-of-everything)

**Defensible claim:** the three-zone logic is a formalisation of an arrival sequence that working hubs already imply — we make it explicit, consistent and tactile across all 68 hubs.

### 5.4 Wayfinding & identity
A network only reads as a network if every hub looks like one. Precedents converge on a **recognisable vertical marker + consistent pictograms + branded palette**:

- Copenhagen: **wayfinding pylons, pictograms + LED, visible ~100 m**. [Intertraffic](https://www.intertraffic.com/news/urban-mobility/at-the-hub-of-everything)
- Munich (MVG *more*) and Vienna (WienMobil): **single brand across many stations**. [VCD – MVG more](https://www.vcd.org/themen/multimodalitaet/beispiele/mvg-more-muenchen) · [Wiener Linien](https://www.wienerlinien.at/wienmobil/stationen)
- Minneapolis: **wayfinding with travel times**, tested for accessibility to as many users as possible. [SUMC – Minneapolis pilot](https://opencommons.org/images/f/f4/Wcmsp-224822.pdf)

**Our equivalents:** the **vertical beacon canopy readable from 50 m**, the **reddish stone-cube paving with embedded directional lines**, and a consistent pictogram/identity marker — the same family as Copenhagen's 100 m pylon, tuned to our finer-grained, tighter-walk network.

## 6. Element / tool-palette logic (what goes at S vs M vs L)

The cross-precedent rule is **baseline element held constant, capability added with tier** — Vienna keeps bike-share everywhere and layers car-share/services on top; Gov.UK adds "extra" then "more" components as type rises. [Wiener Linien](https://www.wienerlinien.at/wienmobil/stationen) · [GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/sustainable-placemaking-guidance-for-mobility-hubs/delivering-quality-mobility-hubs-html)

| Element family | Small (43) | Medium (19) | Large (6) | Precedent basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Micromobility dock (e-bike) | ● core | ● | ● | Vienna baseline bike-share |
| Micro-pod / shuttle-pod stop | ● | ● | ● | our modes; Minneapolis scooter/transit |
| Bench / seating | ● | ● | ● | Minneapolis bench; Gov.UK "core" seating |
| Identity marker / beacon | ● | ● (canopy) | ● (large canopy) | Copenhagen pylon; MVG/Vienna brand |
| Canopy / shelter | minimal | ● | ● | Gov.UK "core" shelter; Copenhagen canopy |
| Real-time info terminal | — | ● | ● | Minneapolis travel-time signage |
| Cargo-bike / e-cargo bay | — | ● | ● | West Midlands cargo bikes |
| Shared-EV bay + charging | — | ● | ● | Bergen/Vienna/Copenhagen EV |
| Autonomous-bus / shuttle interchange | — | (stop) | ● | Minneapolis transit anchoring |
| Cycle repair / service station | — | optional | ● | Vienna bike service; Gov.UK "extra" |
| Parcel lockers | — | optional | ● | Vienna/West Midlands lockers |
| Gastronomy / retail / community | — | — | ● | SUMC hub-as-place; Gov.UK "commercial" |
| Housing above | — | — | ● | our repurposed multi-storey car parks |

**The rationale to defend:** Small hubs are **dwelling + first/last-leg micromobility** at a 150–250 m grain (cheap, replicable, everywhere). Medium hubs add the **decision infrastructure** (info terminal, more modes, charging) that justifies a slightly longer 300–500 m walk. Large hubs add the **commercial/civic programme + housing** that makes them destinations in their own right within the 700–1000 m anchor catchment. This escalation matches Gov.UK's core → extra → more → commercial ladder almost exactly.

### 6.1 Accessibility & inclusive design (must hold at every tier)
- Gov.UK requires, in **all** hub types: **accessible (disabled) parking, real-time information, seating and shelter, lighting, and a high-quality public realm** built on **universal-design principles**. [GOV.UK – Delivering quality: mobility hubs](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/sustainable-placemaking-guidance-for-mobility-hubs/delivering-quality-mobility-hubs-html)
- NACTO: place furniture in the curb buffer to keep a **clear, continuous pedestrian through-zone** — the basis for step-free, unobstructed movement. [NACTO – Bike and Scooter Parking](https://nacto.org/publication/urban-bikeway-design-guide/maintenance-and-operations/bike-and-scooter-parking/)
- SUMC has published a dedicated **women- and caregiver-centred** hub design framework — inclusivity beyond physical access. [SUMC – Women- and caregiver-centered mobility hubs](https://sharedusemobilitycenter.org/shared-use-mobility-center-and-living-cities-and-communities-launch-first-of-its-kind-design-framework-for-women-and-caregiver-centered-mobility-hubs/)

**Our position:** **tactile accessibility throughout** (the embedded directional lines in the paving double as a tactile guidance system), consistent lighting, seating and step-free thresholds at *every* tier — never value-engineered out of the Small hubs.

### 6.2 Modularity & kit-of-parts thinking
Our Rhino **45-block kit-of-parts** is squarely in the mainstream of current hub practice:

- West Midlands: *"a collection of modular components which can be assembled"* from parking bays. [WMCA](https://www.wmca.org.uk/news/new-mobility-hub-set-for-trial-on-the-streets-of-the-west-midlands/)
- Copenhagen: **plug-and-play modules** so municipalities reconfigure layouts as demand evolves. [Intertraffic](https://www.intertraffic.com/news/urban-mobility/at-the-hub-of-everything)
- SANDAG (San Diego) publishes a **Mobility Hub Features Catalog** — an explicit menu of elements assembled per site, the US analogue of our material-organised block library. [SANDAG – Mobility Hub Features Catalog](https://www.sandag.org/projects-and-programs/innovative-mobility/mobility-hubs/-/media/5BBCEF70902542CF820759E1E4921847.ashx)

**Defensible claim:** a modular kit organised by material (pavement, concrete, metal, wood, glass, plastic, greenery, lighting) lets one **design language** flex across 68 context-specific sites — the same strategy WMCA, Copenhagen and SANDAG use, executed as a reusable Rhino block library.

- **Spacing defence:** lead the typology sheets with §3 — the **nested 800 m → 500 m → 250 m radii** argument grounded in CoMoUK's 800 m/10-min catchment. This is the single strongest "why these numbers" slide.
- **VW-gate hub case study:** this is a **Large** hub (repurposed car-park anchor on a transit/autonomous-bus line). Drive it with: (1) the **Bremen economic argument** — hubs relieve far more street than car parks, far cheaper; (2) the **Minneapolis evidence** that transit-anchored hubs + travel-time wayfinding raise usage (64% more likely); (3) the **three-zone arrival sequence (§5.3)** walked through the actual VW-gate geometry (Vehicle Edge at the old ramp → Threshold/beacon at the entry → Dwelling Edge with gastronomy spill-out); (4) **§5.2 hub-as-catalyst** to justify the ground-floor programme + housing above.
- **Accessibility note for every sheet:** cite §6.1 — Gov.UK requires seating, shelter, lighting, accessible parking and real-time info in *all* tiers; our tactile directional paving is the inclusive-design through-line, present even at Small hubs.

# 06 — Arguments & Evidence Bank

**Purpose:** Ready-to-quote arguments for the June 25 studio review and the Aug 16 Wolfsburg Award jury submission. Each argument = (1) a crisp claim, (2) real evidence with a citation URL, (3) a "say it like this" line for the presentation.

**How to read the confidence flags:**
- ✅ **Solid** — primary source, well-established figure.
- ⚠️ **Use with care** — real but contested, range-dependent, or context-specific. Phrasing notes included.

> Quick honesty note for the team: precise per-space, per-car, and "land consumed" numbers vary a lot by country, density, and methodology. Where that's true we say so and give a defensible range instead of a single hero number. Don't quote a number you can't trace back to a source in the list at the bottom.

## 1. Parking eats the city

### 1.1 Parking consumes a huge share of urban land ✅ / ⚠️
**Claim:** Car parking is one of the single largest consumers of urban land — and most of it sits empty most of the time.

| Evidence | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| US: parking spaces per car | ~4 spaces per car (LA County ~3.3) | [Yale Environment Review](https://environment-review.yale.edu/reimagining-parking-unlikely-spaces-climate-resilience) |
| LA County parking footprint | ≈14% of incorporated land area | [Yale Environment Review](https://environment-review.yale.edu/reimagining-parking-unlikely-spaces-climate-resilience) |
| Share of urban land that is parking | >5% of all urban land; up to 25–81% of floor area in dense centres | [Yale Environment Review](https://environment-review.yale.edu/reimagining-parking-unlikely-spaces-climate-resilience) |
| Land per car (US) | ~1,000 sq ft (~93 m²) per car in parking | [CharterUP / Research Institute for Housing America](https://www.charterup.com/blog/a-concrete-perspective-the-troubling-scale-and-enormous-cost-of-parking-lots/) |

> ⚠️ These are mostly **US** figures (4-spaces-per-car culture). For a German audience, frame as "in car-centric cities" rather than implying the exact ratio holds in Wolfsburg. The *direction* is universal; the magnitude is highest in the US.

**Say it like this:** *"For every car, a car-dependent city builds up to four parking spaces — each one roughly the size of a small bedroom. That land sits idle most of the day. In Wolfsburg, with almost one car per resident, that idle land is enormous — and it's exactly the land we reclaim."*

### 1.2 A parking space is expensive infrastructure ✅
**Claim:** A structured parking space is not "free" — it is a €15,000–€35,000 building, before land.

| Type | Cost per space | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Above-grade multi-storey | ~$20,000–$35,000 | [HomeGuide 2026](https://homeguide.com/costs/cost-to-build-a-parking-garage) |
| Multi-level (avg) | ~$19,000–$30,000 | [HomeGuide 2026](https://homeguide.com/costs/cost-to-build-a-parking-garage) |
| Below-grade / underground | $35,000–$100,000 | [HomeGuide 2026](https://homeguide.com/costs/cost-to-build-a-parking-garage) |
| 500-space garage, total | $10.5–15 million | [HomeGuide 2026](https://homeguide.com/costs/cost-to-build-a-parking-garage) |

Germany-specific anchor: in Freiburg-Vauban, a household buys a space in the edge garage for **~$40,000** — bundled with the home ([CSMonitor](https://www.csmonitor.com/2006/1220/p01s03-woeu.html)).

> ⚠️ US construction costs; convert loosely (≈€14k–€30k+ for above-grade). Use the Vauban €/$40k figure when you need a *German* number.

**Say it like this:** *"Every structured parking space is a €20,000–€35,000 building. Our six Large hubs reuse multi-storey car parks that already cost millions to build — instead of demolishing that investment, we repurpose it."*

### 1.3 Minimum parking requirements *manufacture* the demand they claim to serve ✅
**Claim:** Mandatory parking minimums create a self-fulfilling loop of oversupply — Donald Shoup's core finding.

- Free/underpriced parking inflates demand; that inflated demand is then surveyed and used to justify *more* required parking — "circular logic." ([Shoup, *The High Cost of Free Parking*, via Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Shoup))
- Parking requirements can add **more than 10×** the cost of all other public-purpose impact fees combined, and they raise housing prices and drive sprawl. ([Wikipedia: Donald Shoup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Shoup))

**Say it like this:** *"Parking demand isn't a law of nature — it's manufactured by rules that mandate parking, which makes driving easy, which 'proves' we need more parking. Break the loop at the source — replace the parking organising element with mobility hubs — and the demand itself shrinks."*

## 2. The economic case

### 2.1 Car dependency carries large hidden costs that society pays ⚠️
**Claim:** Each private car imposes thousands of euros per year in costs borne by society, not the owner.

| Evidence | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| External cost per car, Europe | ~€2,400–€4,000 / year | [Wikipedia: Externalities of cars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Externalities_of_cars) |
| EU total external cost of car use | ~£303 billion / year (TU Dresden, 2012) | [ETA](https://www.eta.co.uk/news/the-hidden-price-of-car-dependency-why-walking-and-cycling-pay-dividends-for-society) |
| Germany, share of car cost borne by society | 41% / €4,674 per yr (Opel Corsa); 29% / €5,273 per yr (Mercedes GLC), 2020 | [Gössling et al., *The lifetime cost of driving a car*, ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0921800921003943) |
| German household monthly car spend | ~€233/month (vs €33 for public transport) | [Destatis](https://www.destatis.de/EN/Press/2021/07/PE21_N045_639.html) |

> ⚠️ The £303bn EU figure is from a 2012 study — date it. The German per-car societal-cost figures are peer-reviewed (Gössling et al.) and are your strongest economic citation.

**Say it like this:** *"A German study found society pays €4,600–€5,300 per car per year in costs the driver never sees — pollution, noise, crashes, space. Multiply that across Wolfsburg's near-one-car-per-person fleet, and a shared system isn't the expensive option. It's the cheaper one."*

### 2.2 Reclaimed car-land is the project's hidden budget ✅
**Claim:** Land freed from parking is high-value public space — the economic dividend of the transformation.

- Barcelona's full Superblock model was modelled to generate **€1.7 billion/year** in economic savings (mostly health). ([ISGlobal](https://www.isglobal.org/en/-/el-proyecto-original-de-las-supermanzanas-podria-evitar-cerca-de-700-muertes-prematuras-anuales-en-barcelona))
- Bremen's car-sharing relief is valued by the city itself: achieving the same street relief by building residential garages would cost **>€100 million**. ([Share North](https://share-north.eu/2021/05/mission-accomplished-20000-people-in-bremen-are-using-car-sharing-over-6000-cars-replaced-goals-of-the-carsharing-action-plan-achieved/))

**Say it like this:** *"Every parking bay we free is land we don't have to buy — it's already public. That's the hidden budget: we fund public life with land the car was hoarding."*

## 3. Post-car transformation is real, not utopian

### 3.1 Paris is doing it now, at scale ✅
**Claim:** A major capital is removing parking and car streets at scale — and measuring the results.

| Evidence | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| On-street parking to be cut | 72% / ~60,000 spaces | [Park4SUMP](https://park4sump.eu/news-events/news/why-paris-eliminating-72-its-street-parking-spaces) |
| Spaces already removed (6 yrs) | ~24,000 | [Domus](https://www.domusweb.it/en/sustainable-cities/2025/04/23/paris-accelerates-toward-a-car-free-metropolis.html) |
| Streets going car-free (2025 vote) | 500 streets | [Zag Daily](https://zagdaily.com/places/paris-votes-to-make-500-streets-car-free/) |
| New bike lanes (Hidalgo era) | >1,300 km | [Domus](https://www.domusweb.it/en/sustainable-cities/2025/04/23/paris-accelerates-toward-a-car-free-metropolis.html) |
| New parks | 45 hectares | [Domus](https://www.domusweb.it/en/sustainable-cities/2025/04/23/paris-accelerates-toward-a-car-free-metropolis.html) |

**Say it like this:** *"This isn't a thought experiment. Paris is removing 60,000 parking spaces and freeing 500 streets — right now. We're proposing for one company-town what a capital city is already proving works."*

### 3.2 Groningen proved filtered permeability 45+ years ago ✅
**Claim:** Our 5-zone, restricted-entrance circulation model is a proven 1977 strategy, not a gamble. (Direct precedent — cite it as our DNA.)

- 1977 Traffic Circulation Plan split the centre into compartments; cars cross between them only via the ring, bikes move freely. ([Bicycle Dutch](https://bicycledutch.wordpress.com/2016/03/08/groningen-cycling-city-of-the-netherlands/))
- Result: **~60% of all trips by bicycle** — among the highest in the world. ([Bicycle Dutch](https://bicycledutch.wordpress.com/2016/03/08/groningen-cycling-city-of-the-netherlands/))

**Say it like this:** *"Our zone logic isn't invented — it's Groningen 1977. Filter cars, free everything else, and the city flips to 60% cycling. We apply the same logic, with autonomous shared mobility instead of only bikes."*

### 3.3 Germany already has a car-free neighbourhood that works ⚠️
**Claim:** Freiburg-Vauban shows car-light living is achievable *in Germany* — and people give up cars once the system exists.

- Car ownership in Vauban: **~150 cars/1,000 residents**, vs ~430/1,000 in Freiburg overall. ([CSMonitor](https://www.csmonitor.com/2006/1220/p01s03-woeu.html))
- **57%** of families who owned a car when they moved in later gave it up. ([CSMonitor](https://www.csmonitor.com/2006/1220/p01s03-woeu.html))
- Cars park only in edge garages — a direct precedent for our **Vehicle Edge → Threshold → Dwelling Edge** logic.

> ⚠️ Household-level "no car" figures for Vauban are reported inconsistently (40%–70%); lead with the cleaner **150 vs 430 cars/1,000** comparison.

**Say it like this:** *"Freiburg-Vauban already pushes car ownership down to a third of the city average — in Germany, today. Wolfsburg, the literal capital of the car, is the ultimate test of whether that model scales."*

## 5. Why Wolfsburg is the ideal testbed

### 5.1 Wolfsburg has the highest car density in Germany ✅ (hero stat)
**Claim:** Wolfsburg is the most car-saturated city in Germany — which makes it the most meaningful place to prove a post-car model.

- **956 passenger cars per 1,000 residents** (Jan 2025) — **highest in all of Germany**, vs national average 590 and Berlin's 334. ([NWZ Online](https://www.nwzonline.de/panorama/pkw-dichte-in-niedersachsen-wolfsburg-fuehrt-mit-rekordwert_a_4,2,1769393970.html); national avg via [Destatis](https://www.destatis.de/EN/Press/2022/09/PE22_N058_51.html))

**Say it like this:** *"Wolfsburg has almost one car per resident — 956 per 1,000, the highest in Germany. If a post-car city can work *here*, it can work anywhere."*

### 5.2 The VW factory generates predictable, concentrated demand ✅
**Claim:** Shift waves at the world's largest car plant are the perfect prototype for shared autonomous mobility — demand is known in advance.

- Wolfsburg plant: **~60,000 employees**, world's largest single car factory, factory halls covering **1.6 km²** with 75 km of internal roads. ([VW Newsroom](https://www.volkswagen-newsroom.com/en/wolfsburg-plant-the-heart-of-the-vw-brand-6811); [HotCars](https://www.hotcars.com/volkswagen-wolfsburg-plant-detailed-look/))
- Roughly the entire working population of the city (~130,000 residents) is tied to the company or its partners. ([Worldcrunch](https://worldcrunch.com/business-finance/volkswagen-wolfsburg/))

**Say it like this:** *"The shift change is the hardest mobility problem in the city — ~10,000 people moving in one hour — and the easiest to solve with shared fleets, because it's perfectly predictable. We don't guess demand; we know it. That's why the factory gate is our primary case study."*

### 5.3 The symbolism is unbeatable ✅
**Claim:** A post-private-car Wolfsburg is the most powerful possible statement about the car industry's own future.

- Germany's richest city by GDP/capita (~€120,000), built *by and for* the car. ([Worldcrunch](https://worldcrunch.com/business-finance/volkswagen-wolfsburg/))

**Say it like this:** *"There is no more symbolic place on Earth to design a post-private-car city than the hometown of Volkswagen. If the car's birthplace can move beyond private cars, the whole industry's future is on the table."*

Their own study found each shared car replaces 16 private ones, and the network has taken 6,000 cars off the street — relief that would have cost over €100 million in parking garages. Our 68-hub network is that idea, scaled and made autonomous."*

## 7. The AI / computational-method argument

### 7.1 Demand we can model is demand we can right-size ✅
**Claim:** The studio rewards meaningful AI use — and our fleet-sizing problem is exactly what computational mobility modelling is for. The Wolfsburg shift wave is a textbook agent-based-simulation problem.

- Digital twins / agent-based models simulate travel demand and "optimize public transit routes based on real commuter demand data," with adopters cutting peak-corridor congestion **15–20% with no new physical infrastructure**. ([Noobpreneur / digital-twin use cases](https://www.noobpreneur.com/2026/02/21/digital-twin-for-urban-planning-data-tools-and-real-use-cases/))
- Peer-reviewed framework: a digital twin for **urban parking management and mobility forecasting**, using ML to predict parking demand and traffic flow with spatial-temporal models. ([Nature Communications](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-65306-w))
- Agent-based simulation is the established method for modelling individual movement through "extreme urban morphologies" — i.e., exactly a factory-gate shift surge. ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.15143v2))

**How we use AI honestly (our own method, not borrowed claims):**
1. **Demand modelling** — simulate the ~100,000 trips/day and ~9,000 trips/hr peak, including the VW shift wave, to *derive* the 763-vehicle fleet split rather than guessing it.
2. **Hub siting** — compute the three-tier coverage (catchment radii 150–1000 m) so the 68 hubs cover residents, workers, and visitors with minimal gaps.
3. **Agent-based "Anna" journey** — simulate a single worker's home→gate trip to validate that the fleet meets peak demand at the gate hub.

**Say it like this:** *"We didn't pick 763 vehicles by intuition. We modelled the city's trips — including the 10,000-person shift wave — and let the simulation size the fleet and place the hubs. AI here isn't decoration; it's how a predictable demand spike becomes a precisely engineered, right-sized system."*

> ⚠️ The "15–20% congestion cut" stat is from an industry/explainer source, not peer-reviewed — present it as illustrative of what these methods achieve elsewhere, and lead instead with **our own** simulation as the meaningful AI contribution. That's what the studio is grading.

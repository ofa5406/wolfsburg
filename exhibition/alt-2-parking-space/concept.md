# Alternative 2 — "One Parking Space" (purified)

**`<stadt.hub>` · Summaery 2026 · the whole exhibition inside 12.5 m²**

---

## 1. The idea, purified

Alternative 1 arranges stations *around* a taped parking space. Alternative 2 deletes the
stations. There is only the parking space — and **everything fits inside it.**

> **A real 5.0 × 2.5 m parking space, taped on the floor, furnished as a piece of
> reclaimed street: a café table with chairs, plants in pots, a leaning bike, children's
> chalk drawings underfoot, a screen showing the city that makes this normal — and the
> entire exhibition (film, personas, cards, participation) living on and around that
> furniture.**

The exhibit *is* the capacity analysis. The project's method — count what fits, prove it
with numbers — is applied to the exhibit itself: **how much city fits in one parking
space?** Visitors don't read the answer; they sit in it. The card rack is on the café
table. The persona stories run on a tablet propped against the sugar jar. The film loop
plays on the one screen standing where a car's windscreen would be. Kids add drawings to
the chalk field. Nothing exists outside the rectangle except the title wall.

The critique-proof version of the argument: *a parked car serves one person and stands
still 23 hours a day; the same ground, furnished, serves everyone who passes.*

The same ground under a parked car: **12.5 m², one user, ~23 h/day motionless.**
That contrast — printed as a small manifest board leaning against the screen stand —
is the entire text of the exhibit

## 3. How the exhibition content lives inside it

Every deliverable from Alternative 1 survives, but moves *onto the furniture*:

- **The 60-Second City loop** (same storyboard, `../alt-1-stations/draft-content.md` §2)
  → the one screen at the head end. Touch → full deck, 90 s idle → loop. One PC hidden
  under the screen stand or a TV with USB video.
- **Choose a Life personas** → a tablet ON the café table, standing in a cheap holder.
  Visitors sit down to read — the chairs get used, which is the point.
- **Dot-matrix fact cards** → presented as the **café menu**: a menu-card holder on the
  table, "Karte" on the front, the six facts inside as courses. Take-away copies in the
  crate. (Same print files as Alt 1 — only the holder changes.)
- **Persona tickets** → in a napkin holder on the table.
- **Participation** → the **chalk field replaces the sticker map**: "Draw what you'd put
  here." Charcoal/chalk marks accumulate over four days; photograph nightly. (A hopscotch
  outline is pre-drawn in one corner on day 1 — an invitation, and the "kids were here"
  trace you asked for, present from the first hour.)
- **QRs** → a small tent card on the table + one sticker on the bike frame.
- **Title wall** → unchanged from Alt 1 (the one poster), hung behind the head end so
  photos of the space carry the project name.

## 4. Practical judgment calls

- **Charcoal vs. chalk:** real charcoal on a computer-pool floor will smear into the whole
  building. Solution that keeps the aesthetic: tape a **dark board / black paper field**
  (element 3) inside the rectangle and draw with **white + coloured chalk** (or charcoal
  sticks *on the paper only*, fixative-sprayed each evening). Pre-draw the hopscotch and
  one child-style scribble as seeds. Swap the paper when full — full sheets become
  exhibition documentation.
- **Furniture sourcing:** borrow, don't buy — campus café / Werkstatt / flat-shares. One
  table, three mismatched chairs is *better* (reclaimed-street look). Pots from the
  botanical corner or team balconies.
- **The car-shadow trick (cheap, strong):** before furnishing, draw the outline of a car
  (4.5 × 1.8 m) in white tape *inside* the space, like a crime-scene silhouette. The café
  sits on top of the "car". Visitors read the double image instantly.
- **Liability/food:** no real coffee service — empty cups + a carafe as props (or water
  cups if the pool rules allow). The menu is the exhibition menu, not a real one.
- **Supervision:** still fully unattended. Everything is either taped down, heavy, or
  deliberately takeable (cards). Tablet in kiosk mode + cable lock.

Alt 2 frees **both projectors and two tablets** for the other four class projects — worth
saying out loud in the equipment negotiation.

## 6. Alt 1 vs Alt 2 — decision aid

| | Alt 1 · Station constellation | Alt 2 · One Parking Space |
|---|---|---|
| Core image | Room orbits an empty taped space | The space itself, furnished and alive |
| Screens needed | 2 PCs + TV + tablet + projector | 1 screen + 1 tablet |
| Build effort | Loop + projection cycle + prints | Loop + prints + **furniture logistics** |
| Risk | Projector not granted; more tech to babysit | Furniture sourcing; chalk mess management |
| Visitor role | Watches, explores, then acts | **Sits inside the argument immediately** |
| Photo that travels | Projected rectangle | People drinking "coffee" in a parking space |
| Purity of thesis | Explains reclaiming | **Performs reclaiming** |

They also combine: Alt 2 as built reality + Alt 1's projector cycle aimed at the chalk
field after dark (Summaery evenings) is the maximal version if equipment allows.

# Moodboard — Real Precedents for Both Alternatives

Every layer of our exhibit has a famous, documented real-world ancestor. Use this file two
ways: as a **defense** ("this sits in a known lineage") and as a **visual moodboard** —
each entry ends with image-search keywords; drop them into Google Images / Pinterest /
Are.na and collect the board from the linked projects' own photos.

One sentence that frames the whole lineage for tutors:

> *PARK(ing) Day proved a parking space can hold a park for two hours; parklets made it
> permanent; `<stadt.hub>` shows the system that makes it citywide.*

---

## A · The furnished parking space *(Alt 2 core)*

**PARK(ing) Day — Rebar, San Francisco, 2005.**
On Nov 16, 2005, the Rebar collective fed a parking meter and installed sod, a rented park
bench and a 15-foot tree in the space — a legal park for exactly as long as the meter ran.
Published as an open-source manual; now an annual event in hundreds of cities.
→ [myparkingday.org/about](https://www.myparkingday.org/about) ·
[John Bela (Rebar)](https://www.johnbela.com/work/parking-day) ·
[Next City interview](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/hacking-public-space-designers-parking-day)
**Steal:** the meter-fed legality wit; the grass-bench-tree minimal kit; "roll up the sod
and leave it clean."
**Images:** `PARK(ing) Day 2005 Rebar first installation sod bench tree`

**San Francisco parklets ("Pavement to Parks", 2010→).**
The city institutionalised PARK(ing) Day into permanent café decks and mini-parks in former
parking spaces — the "after" state of our own proposal.
→ [Fast Company history](https://www.fastcompany.com/90730521/from-one-parking-spot-to-100-public-parks-the-history-of-san-franciscos-street-transformation)
**Steal:** platform-with-planters framing; how a parklet edge signals "public, sit down."
**Images:** `San Francisco parklet pavement to parks café deck`

**German PARKing-Day "Wohnzimmer" actions (Berlin, Stuttgart, everywhere).**
Sofas, carpets, hammocks and armchairs placed in parking spaces for a day — the local,
grassroots version our tutors will know first-hand.
→ [PARKing Day Berlin](https://www.parking-day-berlin.de/) ·
[example press](https://www.landeszeitung.de/lokales/33765-1577831/) ·
[BUNDjugend how-to](https://www.bundjugend-bw.de/aktionstipp-parking-day-organisieren/)
**Steal:** the domestic-furniture surrealism; registration/permit pragmatics (§ our
checklist's "check pool rules").
**Images:** `Parking Day Deutschland Wohnzimmer Parkplatz Sofa`

**Better Block Project (Dallas, 2010→) · Raumlabor Berlin · Barcelona Superblocks (Poblenou).**
Overnight pop-up street transformations with borrowed café furniture, planters and paint
(Better Block); temporary architecture as urban experiment, the German art-school reference
(Raumlabor); municipal tactical pilots with tables in former roadway (Superblocks).
**Steal:** the borrowed-mismatched-furniture aesthetic (our "3 mismatched chairs is
better"); paint/tape as legitimate design material.
**Images:** `Better Block before after pop-up street` · `Raumlabor Berlin temporary
installation` · `superilla Poblenou tactical urbanism paint planters`

## B · The space arithmetic *(Alt 2 manifest board · Alt 1 loop + dot-matrix cards)*

**The Münster poster, 1991.**
The city photographed the same street three times — filled by 72 bicycles, 72 cars, and
one bus. The most reproduced single image in mobility advocacy, recreated worldwide.
→ [dataphys history](http://dataphys.org/list/congestion-visualization/) ·
[the photo](https://www.flickr.com/photos/carltonreid/6440857817) ·
[Canberra recreation](https://danielbowen.com/2012/09/19/road-space-photo/)
**Steal:** same-frame before/after comparison; space (not speed) as the argument — exactly
our "yesterday vs. today, 12.5 m²" board and the dot-matrix grammar.
**Images:** `Münster 1991 Fahrrad Auto Bus Platzbedarf Vergleich`

**"The Arrogance of Space" — Mikael Colville-Andersen.**
Colour-overlay diagrams exposing how much street area is allocated to cars vs. people.
**Steal:** the flat colour-block overlay language for the car-shadow tape and any
street-section graphics.
**Images:** `arrogance of space Colville-Andersen overlay`

## C · The participation layer *(Alt 2 chalk field · Alt 1 sticker map)*

**Candy Chang — "Before I Die" (New Orleans, 2011).**
A chalkboard wall on an abandoned house with the stencil *"Before I die I want to ___"*;
filled within a day, replicated on 5,000+ walls in ~70 countries. The proof that a chalk
prompt + public surface = self-sustaining participation.
→ [project page](https://www.candychang.com/beforeidie/) ·
[Marginalian essay](https://www.themarginalian.org/2013/11/05/before-i-die-a-candy-chang/)
**Steal:** the fill-in-the-blank prompt structure (our *"Draw what you'd put here"*);
chalkboard paint; photographing the accumulating wall as the artwork.
**Images:** `Candy Chang Before I Die wall chalk`

**Candy Chang — "I Wish This Was" (New Orleans, 2010).**
Thousands of name-tag-style vinyl stickers (*"I wish this was ___"*) on vacant storefronts;
answers ranged from "a bakery" to "heaven." Direct ancestor of our sticker map.
→ [project page](https://www.candychang.com/i-was-this-was/) ·
[NPR feature](https://www.npr.org/2012/07/02/155916382/filling-in-new-orleans-future-one-blank-at-a-time)
**Steal:** sticker-as-invitation; keep the prompt warm and open, never clinical.
**Images:** `Candy Chang I Wish This Was stickers storefront`

## D · The unattended interactive station *(Alt 1 core)*

**MIT CityScope "FindingPlaces" — Hamburg, 2016.**
Interactive projected city model (maps projected onto a table of tagged Lego blocks);
~500 citizens in dozens of workshops placed blocks to propose 160 accepted sites for
refugee housing. The academic gold standard for "place a thing on a projected map and the
city responds" — our projector cycle and hub-placement participation in one precedent,
from a German city.
→ [MIT Media Lab overview](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/cityscope-hamburg/overview/) ·
[findingplaces.hamburg](https://findingplaces.hamburg/hintergrund/so-funktioniert-das-modell.html) ·
[OECD case study](https://oecd-opsi.org/innovations/cityscope-findingplaces-hci-platform-for-public-participation-in-refugees-accommodation-process/)
**Steal:** projection onto a horizontal surface people gather around; participation that
produces data (photograph the map nightly).
**Images:** `CityScope FindingPlaces Hamburg projected model Lego`

**MOIA "presenter mode" (already our reference — `research/10_moia-mia-precedent.md`).**
The full-screen, low-text scenario view built for unattended display — the direct template
for the 60-Second City loop's behaviour (attract → touch → explore → idle → attract).
**Steal:** one message per screen; interface only appears on interaction.

**Autostadt, Wolfsburg (VW's own brand-land).**
Twenty years of polished, self-explanatory mobility exhibits five hundred metres from our
site. Visit-able as a live masterclass in unattended exhibition craft — and the productive
irony writes its own wall text: *the company that perfected exhibiting the car provides
the standard for exhibiting its retirement.*
**Steal:** label discipline (title → one line → object); floor graphics as wayfinding.
**Images:** `Autostadt Wolfsburg Ausstellung exhibit design`

2. Lay out on one A3 (or a shared Are.na/Pinterest board) in the four groups A–D above,
   each group captioned with its "steal" line.
3. Print one copy for the July 3 team meeting — it doubles as the tutors' evidence that
   the exhibit design is research-based, and as a checklist of visual standards to hit.
